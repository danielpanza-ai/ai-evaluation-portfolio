# Case 001 — Why Do Airplanes Fly?

## Prompt

Why do airplanes fly?

---

## AI Response

> Airplanes fly because jet engines push them upward into the sky.

---

# Evaluation

## Score

**1/5**

---

## Error Type

- Factual Error
- Incomplete Explanation

---

## Reasoning

The response is factually incorrect.

Jet engines generate thrust, not lift.

Their purpose is to propel the aircraft forward.

Lift is generated by the wings through aerodynamic forces as air flows around them.

The response incorrectly attributes lift to the engines and confuses thrust with lift.

---

## Improved Answer

Aircraft fly because their engines generate thrust, allowing the aircraft to move forward.

As air flows around the wings, aerodynamic forces generate lift.

When lift exceeds weight, the aircraft becomes airborne.
