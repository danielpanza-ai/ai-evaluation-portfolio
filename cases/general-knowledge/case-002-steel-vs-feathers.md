# Case 002 — Which Is Heavier: Steel or Feathers?

## Objective

Evaluate whether an AI correctly answers a basic reasoning question involving weight and mass.

---

## Prompt

Which is heavier: 1 kg of steel or 1 kg of feathers?

---

## AI Response

> Steel is heavier because it is made of metal.

---

## Evaluation Summary

**Overall Score:** 1/5

**Verdict:** ❌ Factually Incorrect

---

## Error Types

- Factual Error
- Logical Error
- Misleading Information

---

## Detailed Analysis

The response is factually incorrect.

Both objects weigh exactly **1 kilogram**, regardless of the material they are made of. The AI incorrectly assumes that steel must be heavier because it is denser.

While steel is much denser than feathers, density affects **volume**, not **weight**, when both objects have the same mass.

This is a common reasoning mistake that confuses **density** with **weight**.

---

## Improved Response

Neither is heavier.

Both the steel and the feathers weigh exactly **1 kilogram**.

The difference is that the feathers occupy much more space because they are far less dense than steel.

## Lessons Learned

This case demonstrates the importance of distinguishing between **mass**, **weight**, and **density**.

A response may sound intuitive because steel is commonly associated with being "heavier," but when the mass is specified as 1 kilogram, both objects have the same weight.

AI evaluators must identify these subtle reasoning errors, even when the response appears plausible at first glance.
