# Case 003 — Who Discovered Brazil?

## Objective

Evaluate whether an AI provides a historically accurate answer about the European arrival in Brazil.

---

## Prompt

Who discovered Brazil?

---

## AI Response

> Christopher Columbus discovered Brazil in 1492.

---

## Evaluation Summary

**Overall Score:** 1/5

**Verdict:** ❌ Factually Incorrect

---

## Error Types

- Factual Error
- Historical Inaccuracy
- Misleading Information

---

## Detailed Analysis

The response is factually incorrect.

Christopher Columbus never reached Brazil. In 1492, he arrived in the Caribbean while sailing under the Spanish Crown.

According to the traditional historical account, Brazil was officially reached by the Portuguese explorer **Pedro Álvares Cabral** in **1500**.

The response also provides an incorrect date and incorrectly attributes the European arrival in Brazil to Columbus.

Additionally, the wording "discovered Brazil" oversimplifies the historical context because Indigenous peoples had inhabited the region for thousands of years before European arrival.

---

## Improved Response

According to the traditional historical account, Brazil was officially reached by the Portuguese explorer **Pedro Álvares Cabral** in **1500**.

However, it is important to recognize that millions of Indigenous people had lived in the region for thousands of years before the arrival of Europeans. For this reason, many historians prefer the expression **"European arrival in Brazil"** rather than "discovery."

## Why This Matters for AI Training

Historical questions require more than factual accuracy. AI systems should avoid reinforcing outdated or overly simplistic narratives when additional context improves accuracy and neutrality.

This case demonstrates the importance of verifying historical facts, dates, and terminology before presenting information as definitive.

---

## Lessons Learned

This evaluation highlights the importance of checking multiple aspects of an AI response rather than focusing on a single fact.

The response contained several independent errors:
- The wrong historical figure.
- The wrong year.
- An oversimplified historical interpretation.

AI evaluators should identify every significant issue in a response and provide a corrected version that is both accurate and balanced.
